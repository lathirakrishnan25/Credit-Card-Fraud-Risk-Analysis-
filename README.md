📊 Project Overview
Vigilance-550 is a comprehensive forensic data analysis project involving over 550,000+ bank transactions spanning from 1995 to 2021. The goal was to move banking security from a reactive state (responding to fraud after it happens) to a proactive state (predicting and preventing fraud in real-time).

Through two distinct dashboards, this project uncovers a "Perfect Storm" of fraud risk: Online Shopping transactions occurring at 11:00 PM during the month of August.

🚀 Key Features
1. The Tactical Map (Dashboard 1)
Focuses on the Who, Where, and What.

Geographic Clusters: Identifies North American fraud hotspots (e.g., Camden and Birmingham).

High-Risk Categories: Isolates shopping_net as the primary weapon for fraudsters.

Merchant Risk Scoring: Ranks specific merchants (e.g., Mosciski Group) with deviant transaction patterns.

2. The Trend & Pattern Analysis (Dashboard 2)
Focuses on the When and How.

Temporal Risk Profile: A 24-hour velocity chart revealing a massive spike at 23:00 (11:00 PM).

Multi-Year Decomposition: Breaks down ₹1.13M in total fraud by year, month, and category.

Hourly Tooltips: Integrated Pie Chart tooltips that show the categorical anatomy of every hourly spike.🔍 Final Conclusion & Business Insights

🔍 Final Conclusion & Business Insights
The audit proves that fraud is predictable, not random.

The Root Cause: Thieves utilize automated bots late at night to exploit online shopping platforms with weak security.

Seasonality: Fraud risk builds from June and peaks in August.

The Solution:

Night Shift Security: Require MFA (Text Codes) for all online orders between 10 PM and Midnight.

August Alert: Lower auto-decline thresholds during high-risk months.

Merchant Watch: Apply extra verification layers to high-risk merchants identified in the dashboard.
